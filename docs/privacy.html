Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 24, 2026
Last updated: May 24, 2026

At a glance

Catchly is a browser extension that helps you keep track of your subscriptions. It was built around a simple architectural decision: we do not see your data.

  • Your subscription information is stored locally in your browser, in chrome.storage.local on your device. It never leaves your computer.
  • There is no account system. You do not create a login, provide your name, or set a password.
  • There is no backend server that holds your subscription information. We could not look up your subscriptions if we wanted to, because the infrastructure does not exist.
  • The only personal information we collect is your email address — and only if you voluntarily join our waitlist for an upcoming feature.
  • We do not use third-party analytics, advertising trackers, session-replay tools, or any tools that track your behavior. We do not share or sell your data.

The full policy below provides the specifics of what we collect, what we do not, your rights, and how to contact us.

1. About Catchly #

Catchly ("we," "us," "our") is a browser extension product. The Catchly service includes the Chrome extension, the website at getcatchly.com, and any related services we operate.

Catchly is developed and operated by an independent software developer based in India. The product is offered globally, with a primary user base in the United States.

For all privacy-related contact:

Email: privacy@getcatchly.com

We aim to respond to privacy inquiries within 14 days. If you do not receive a response, please check your spam folder and try again.

2. Information we collect #

This is the most important section of this policy. We are specific because specificity is the point.

2.1 Information stored locally on your device

The Catchly extension stores the following information in chrome.storage.local on your device. This information does not leave your browser. We do not have access to it.

Subscription records you create. When you add a subscription manually, or accept one detected by the extension, we save a record containing: the service name, the amount and currency, the billing cycle, the next renewal date, the date you started the subscription, the category you assign, any notes you write, and the source URL where the subscription was detected (if applicable).

Usage signals for shadow-charge detection. To warn you about renewals on services you have stopped using, the extension records timestamps of when you last visited each tracked service. These timestamps stay on your device.

Settings and preferences. Your reminder schedule, default currency, theme preference, and notification settings.

Event log. A short rolling log of events the extension has handled, such as price changes detected or trials approaching their end. Capped at 500 entries.

Pending captures. When the extension detects a possible subscription, the detection is held briefly while you decide whether to confirm or dismiss it.

All of the above is stored locally and is not transmitted to us or any third party.

2.2 Information we collect through the waitlist

If you voluntarily submit your email address through the waitlist signup form, we collect:

  • Your email address
  • A short label indicating which surface you signed up from (such as "welcome screen," "settings," or "landing page")
  • The version of the extension you were using at signup, if applicable
  • The timestamp of your signup

This information is transmitted to and stored in Cloudflare Workers KV, a key-value storage service operated by Cloudflare, Inc.

We use this information solely to notify you when the relevant upcoming feature launches. After we send that notification, we will not contact you further unless you separately opt in to additional communications.

You may request deletion of your waitlist record at any time by emailing privacy@getcatchly.com with the subject "Delete my waitlist signup" and the email address you submitted.

2.3 Information we do not collect

We want to be specific about what is absent, because for a privacy-focused product, the absences matter as much as the presences.

  • Financial information. We do not collect or have access to bank accounts, credit card numbers, debit cards, or any financial credentials. The extension does not request this information and has no infrastructure to handle it.
  • Account information. There is no Catchly account system. We do not collect names, passwords, dates of birth, or any identifiers required to create or log into an account.
  • Browsing history. The extension does not record, store, or transmit the URLs you visit or the pages you browse. The content script briefly examines page-level signals to determine whether a page appears to be a subscription sign-up flow; it does not maintain a record of which pages you visit.
  • Application analytics. We do not use Google Analytics, Plausible, Fathom, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any similar analytics platform.
  • Advertising trackers. We do not use advertising networks, tracking pixels, conversion tags, or remarketing technology.
  • Session-replay or behavioral analytics. We do not use Hotjar, FullStory, LogRocket, or any tool that records user sessions or interactions.
  • Error reporting (currently). We do not currently use any error-reporting or crash-tracking service such as Sentry, Bugsnag, or Rollbar. If we ever add such a service in the future, it will be opt-in only, will require your explicit consent through an in-app prompt, and will transmit only technical diagnostic information (such as stack traces, browser version, and extension version) — never subscription data, email content, or personal information.
  • Network-layer information from our application. Our Cloudflare Worker code does not access, read, or persist IP addresses, user-agent strings, or other network metadata. However, as is standard for any internet service, Cloudflare's edge infrastructure logs certain network metadata, including IP addresses, for security, routing, and abuse prevention purposes. These logs are governed by Cloudflare's data practices, not ours. See Cloudflare's privacy policy at cloudflare.com/privacypolicy for their handling. We do not query, use, or analyze these logs as a matter of policy.

2.4 Information sold or shared

We do not sell or share your personal information for any purpose, including for cross-context behavioral advertising as that term is defined under the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA).

We have not sold or shared personal information in the past twelve months.

4. Browser extension permissions #

Browser extensions must declare permissions in their manifest.json file. The Chrome Web Store displays these permissions when you install the extension. Here is what each one does and why we request it:

storage — Saves your subscription data, settings, and preferences to your device using chrome.storage.local. The extension cannot function without this permission.

alarms — Schedules renewal reminders via Chrome's alarm system. Required for the notification timing logic.

notifications — Displays desktop notifications when a renewal is approaching, a trial is ending, or a shadow-charge warning is triggered.

tabs — Opens new tabs when you click a notification or a cancellation link from the extension popup.

Host permissions — narrow allowlist, not <all_urls>

The extension declares a fixed allowlist of host permissions covering only the specific subscription services it supports — currently 24 domains, including netflix.com, spotify.com, disneyplus.com, max.com, hbomax.com, hulu.com, primevideo.com, youtube.com, music.apple.com, tv.apple.com, chatgpt.com, openai.com, claude.ai, anthropic.com, notion.so, notion.com, grammarly.com, dropbox.com, 1password.com, adobe.com, audible.com, nytimes.com, github.com, and figma.com. Every host is enumerated explicitly in the published manifest.json file in the source repository.

The browser itself enforces this list. Catchly cannot run on any site outside it — including your bank, brokerage, healthcare portal, payment processor, identity provider, employer SSO, or government login. That's a platform-level restriction in Chrome, not a "trust us" promise.

On the allowlisted services, the content script's only purpose is subscription detection. When you visit a recognized service, the script briefly examines signals such as the URL, page title, and a small portion of visible text to determine whether the page appears to be a subscription sign-up or checkout flow. This examination happens entirely within your browser. If the page does not appear to be a subscription flow, the script takes no action.

The content script does not:

  • Read or transmit the contents of forms you fill out
  • Read or transmit passwords, payment information, or authentication credentials
  • Maintain a record of pages you visit
  • Transmit page content, URLs, or browsing patterns to any external server
  • Track you across websites

When a new subscription service is added to a future release, the host-permission allowlist is extended in that release's manifest and the change is visible as a diff in the public source repository. Adding a service is the only way Catchly can come to read pages on it.

5. Cookies and tracking technologies #

The Catchly extension does not use cookies. The extension does not create persistent identifiers that allow us to recognize you across sessions or devices.

The Catchly website (getcatchly.com) does not use third-party tracking cookies, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or advertising-network tracking technology. We use browser localStorage only to remember your theme preference (light or dark mode). This use is considered strictly necessary for the website to function as designed and does not require separate consent under the EU ePrivacy Directive or comparable regulations.

If we add server-side analytics in the future, we will use a privacy-preserving solution that does not track individual visitors (such as Plausible, Fathom, or Cloudflare's privacy-focused analytics) and we will update this policy to reflect that change.

6. How we handle the data we do collect #

The only personal information we collect is the waitlist email addresses described in Section 2.2. Here is how that information is handled:

Service provider. Waitlist emails are stored in Cloudflare Workers KV, operated by Cloudflare, Inc. Cloudflare acts as a service provider (as that term is used under the CCPA/CPRA) and a data processor (as that term is used under GDPR) for the limited purpose of operating our waitlist endpoint. Cloudflare's processing is governed by Cloudflare's standard data processing terms, which restrict them from using your information for any purpose other than providing the requested service.

Storage location. Cloudflare operates a globally distributed infrastructure. Your email address may be stored or processed in data centers located in the United States, Europe, or other countries where Cloudflare operates. Cloudflare's privacy practices are documented at cloudflare.com/privacypolicy.

For users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland. International transfers of your email address to Cloudflare are governed by Cloudflare's data transfer arrangements, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission, where applicable. By submitting your email to our waitlist, you consent to this transfer.

Encryption. Data is encrypted in transit (via TLS) and at rest (via Cloudflare's standard infrastructure encryption).

Access. Only the Catchly developer has administrative access to the waitlist data. No third parties access the data.

Retention. Waitlist emails are retained until the relevant upcoming feature launches and the launch notification has been sent. After that, emails are retained for an additional 90 days, then deleted.

Maximum retention. Regardless of launch status, waitlist emails will be deleted no later than 24 months from the date of collection.

Deletion requests. Earlier deletion may be requested at any time by emailing privacy@getcatchly.com.

7. Your privacy rights #

For US residents — California (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act:

  • Right to know. You may request information about the categories of personal information we have collected, the sources of that information, the purposes for collection, the categories of third parties (if any) with whom we share information, and the specific pieces of personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to delete. You may request deletion of personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain legal exceptions.
  • Right to correct. You may request correction of inaccurate personal information we hold about you.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing. We do not sell or share personal information. There is nothing to opt out of, but this right exists if our practices change.
  • Right to limit use of sensitive personal information. We do not collect sensitive personal information as that term is defined under the CPRA.
  • Right to non-discrimination. We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights.

To exercise your California rights, email privacy@getcatchly.com. We will verify your request and respond within 45 days, with one possible 45-day extension if reasonably necessary.

California Shine the Light disclosure. California residents may request information about disclosures of personal information to third parties for direct marketing purposes. We do not engage in such disclosures.

For US residents — other state laws

If you reside in Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, or other US states with comprehensive privacy laws, you have rights substantially similar to those described above for California residents, including rights of access, deletion, correction, and (where applicable) the right to opt out of profiling and targeted advertising. We do not engage in profiling or targeted advertising. To exercise any state-specific rights, email privacy@getcatchly.com.

For users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland (GDPR / UK GDPR)

If you are located in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under the General Data Protection Regulation:

  • Right of access. Confirm whether we process your personal data and obtain a copy.
  • Right to rectification. Correct inaccurate personal data.
  • Right to erasure. Have your personal data deleted (the "right to be forgotten").
  • Right to restrict processing. Limit how we process your data.
  • Right to data portability. Receive your data in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to object. Object to certain types of processing.
  • Right to withdraw consent. Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.
  • Right to lodge a complaint. Lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

The legal basis for processing your waitlist email address is your explicit consent, given when you submit your email. You may withdraw this consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing prior to withdrawal.

To exercise these rights, email privacy@getcatchly.com. We will respond within 30 days.

For users in India (DPDP Act, 2023)

If you are located in India, you have rights under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, including the right to access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, and nomination. To exercise these rights, email privacy@getcatchly.com. The Data Fiduciary contact and Grievance Officer details are available upon request via email.

For users elsewhere

We aim to respect the privacy rights of all users regardless of jurisdiction. If you have a privacy concern, contact us at the email address above and we will work with you to address it.

8. Children's privacy #

The Catchly service is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from:

  • Children under 13, as that term is defined under the United States Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA);
  • Minors under 16, as that term is defined under the California Privacy Rights Act and various EU member state implementations of GDPR; or
  • Children under 18, as that term is defined under India's DPDP Act 2023.

If you believe a child has submitted personal information to Catchly, please contact privacy@getcatchly.com and we will delete the record promptly.

Parents and guardians of minors who have submitted information may request deletion at the same email address.

9. Data security #

The Catchly service is designed to minimize the data security risk surface by minimizing the data we collect in the first place. There is no central database of user subscriptions to be breached, because that database does not exist.

For the limited personal information we do collect — waitlist email addresses stored in Cloudflare KV — we rely on Cloudflare's security infrastructure, including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, and SOC 2 compliance.

Notification of security incidents. In the unlikely event of a security incident affecting personal information we hold, we will notify affected users without undue delay and within the timeframes required by applicable law (within 72 hours for GDPR-covered users; as soon as reasonably practicable for users in jurisdictions with similar requirements; in accordance with applicable US state breach notification laws for US users).

No software service can guarantee absolute security. You can help protect your data by keeping your browser and operating system up to date and enabling full-disk encryption on your device.

10. International data transfers #

As described in Section 6, our service provider Cloudflare operates a global infrastructure, and your waitlist email may be processed in data centers outside your country of residence. We rely on Cloudflare's standard contractual arrangements, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission, to govern international transfers where applicable.

By submitting your email to our waitlist, you consent to the international transfer of that information.
