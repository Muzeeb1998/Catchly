Privacy by architecture

Your subscriptions never leave your browser.

Catchly stores everything in chrome.storage.local — your subscriptions live on your device, not on a server. There's no backend. No account. No database. We don't see your data because we don't have it. The architecture makes it impossible. The code is open source on GitHub — anyone can read it and verify the privacy claims for themselves. The strongest privacy proof isn't a marketing claim — it's the absence of the systems that would make a leak possible.

Why Catchly exists

Most subscription trackers solve the wrong problem.

The category was built on a tradeoff nobody actually wanted. Catchly was built on the opposite one.

Bank-linked subscription trackers and the category they built

  • Require your bank login through Plaid or similar
  • Store your financial data on their servers
  • Need an account, email, and password before you can even try the product
  • Take 30–60% of any savings they negotiate on your behalf
  • Send marketing emails you didn't ask for
  • Share data with "partners" buried deep in their privacy policy
  • Charge a recurring monthly fee just to show you what you're already paying for
  • Stop working the moment your bank changes their API
  • Can't detect a subscription until after it's hit your statement
  • Often harder to cancel than the subscriptions they're tracking
